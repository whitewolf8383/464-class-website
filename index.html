Project Details

Our project focuses on student interaction within the classroom. Upon our investigations into classroom particiapation studies, we found that less then 10% of the student class population regularly participate during the class. Additionally, we found that as class sizes get larger, as typical in today's college classrooms, the participation rate decreases even further. We also discovered that those students who activily participate during classes tend to recieve higher class grades and a better understanding of the material being presented.

Our focus for this project is to create a means that allows students to participate in class activities throughout the entire class period, but not being forced into a situation in which they may feel unsecure by speaking or participating in front of the entire group. Additionally, we wanted a means for students to be able to revisit information during the class period. Finally, our investigation into research conducted on student performance has shown that students who regularly quiz themselves over recent informaiton tend to learn the information at a faster rate. Therefore, we incorporated a testing process to allow students to test shortly after learning the new information in order to better strengthen their memory of the subject matter.
